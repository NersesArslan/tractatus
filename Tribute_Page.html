Ludwig Wittgenstein

Wittgenstein's Life and Work

Ludwig Witttgenstein was an Austrian-British philosopher who worked in logic, philosophy of mathematics, philosophy of mind and philosophy of language.

  • Born on April 26 1889 in Neuwaldegg, Austria.
  • Started studying mechanical engineering at the Technische Hochschule Berlin in 1906.
  • Arrived at Cambridge to study foundations of mathematics under Bertrand Russell in 1911.
  • Retreated to Skolden Norway to write Notes on Logic in 1913.
  • Enlisted in the Austro-Hungarian Army at the outbreak of WWI (1914).
  • Published the Tractatus in 1921.
  • Designed Haus Wittgenstein for his sister Gretl in 1928.
  • Appointed as Lecturer at Cambridge in 1929.
  • Elected as chair of philosophy in 1939.
  • Resigned his professorship to focus on his writings in 1947.
  • Passed away leaving behind voluminous unpublished manuscripts in 1951.
  • Publication of Philosophical Investigations.

From the Tractatus:

"Death is not an event in life: We do not live to experience death. If we take eternity to mean not infinite temporal duration but timelessness, then eternal life belongs to those who live in the present. Our life has no end in the way in which our visual field has no limits."
